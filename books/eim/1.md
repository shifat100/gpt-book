## 1.1 Overview of Export-Import Business

### 1.1.1 Meaning and Definition

**Exporting** refers to the process of selling goods or services produced in one country to buyers located in another country. It involves compliance with domestic regulations, international trade laws, and the commercial practices of the destination market.

**Importing** is the process of bringing goods or services from a foreign country into the domestic market for consumption, resale, or further processing.

Together, export-import management encompasses the planning, organization, execution, and control of all activities involved in cross-border trade transactions.

### 1.1.2 Objectives of Exporting for Firms

Firms engage in export activities to achieve several strategic objectives:

**Revenue Expansion**
- Access to larger international markets beyond domestic boundaries
- Increased sales volume and revenue generation
- Reduction of dependence on domestic market fluctuations

**Market Diversification**
- Risk mitigation through geographic diversification
- Reduced vulnerability to local economic downturns
- Seasonal demand balancing across different markets

**Competitive Advantage**
- Economies of scale through higher production volumes
- Enhanced brand recognition and global presence
- Learning from international best practices and innovation

**Resource Optimization**
- Better utilization of production capacity
- Extended product life cycles in new markets
- Improved return on investment in production facilities

**Strategic Positioning**
- Establishment as a global player in the industry
- Strengthened bargaining power with suppliers
- Enhanced credibility and reputation

### 1.1.3 Advantages of Exporting

**Financial Benefits**
- Higher profit margins in certain foreign markets
- Access to foreign currency earnings
- Eligibility for government export incentives and subsidies
- Tax benefits and duty drawback facilities

**Business Growth**
- Rapid business expansion without significant capital investment
- Extension of product life cycle
- Increased production efficiency through larger orders

**Knowledge Acquisition**
- Exposure to international quality standards
- Learning advanced production techniques
- Understanding global consumer preferences
- Development of international business acumen

**Competitive Strengthening**
- Enhanced competitiveness through international exposure
- Innovation driven by diverse market requirements
- Stronger negotiation position in domestic market

### 1.1.4 Risks Associated with Exporting

**Financial Risks**
- Exchange rate fluctuations affecting profitability
- Payment default or non-payment by foreign buyers
- Increased working capital requirements
- Cost of international trade finance

**Operational Risks**
- Complex documentation and regulatory compliance
- Longer payment cycles compared to domestic trade
- Quality control challenges across distances
- Logistics and transportation complications

**Market Risks**
- Political instability in target markets
- Changes in foreign trade policies and tariffs
- Cultural misunderstandings affecting business relationships
- Intense competition from local and international players

**Legal and Regulatory Risks**
- Compliance with multiple jurisdictions' laws
- Intellectual property protection challenges
- Dispute resolution complexities
- Changing import regulations in destination countries

## 1.3 Support Systems: Service Institutions for Exporters and Importers

Bangladesh has established a comprehensive ecosystem of institutions to support and facilitate international trade. Understanding the role of these organizations is crucial for successful export-import operations.

### 1.3.1 Government Institutions

**Export Promotion Bureau (EPB)**

*Role and Functions:*
- Promotes Bangladeshi products in international markets
- Organizes participation in international trade fairs and exhibitions
- Provides market information and trade statistics
- Issues Export Registration Certificate (ERC)
- Facilitates buyer-seller meetings and trade delegations
- Maintains database of exporters and products

*Services for Exporters:*
- Market research reports on various countries and products
- Trade enquiries and buyer information
- Export facilitation services
- Training programs on export procedures
- Online ERC application and renewal system

**Office of the Chief Controller of Imports & Exports (CCI&E)**

*Role and Functions:*
- Regulatory authority for import and export trade
- Issues Import Registration Certificate (IRC)
- Monitors compliance with import-export policies
- Administers import-export control mechanisms
- Maintains national import-export database
- Enforces Foreign Trade Policy

*Services Provided:*
- IRC issuance and renewal
- Import policy guidance and clarification
- Regulatory compliance assistance
- Trade statistics and documentation

**Bangladesh Bank (Central Bank)**

*Role and Functions:*
- Formulates foreign exchange policies
- Regulates international trade transactions
- Issues guidelines for opening Letters of Credit (L/C)
- Monitors export proceeds realization
- Administers export incentive schemes
- Ensures compliance with foreign exchange regulations

*Services for Trade:*
- Foreign exchange management
- L/C authorization and monitoring
- Export financing schemes
- Remittance facilitation
- Trade-related banking guidelines

**National Board of Revenue (NBR)**

*Role and Functions:*
- Administers customs duties and taxes
- Implements duty drawback and VAT rebate schemes
- Monitors import-export through customs procedures
- Issues VAT registration certificates
- Enforces customs laws and regulations

*Services Provided:*
- Customs clearance procedures
- Duty assessment and collection
- Tax exemption and rebate processing
- VAT registration and compliance

**Bangladesh Investment Development Authority (BIDA)**

*Role and Functions:*
- Facilitates business registration and licensing
- Provides one-stop service for investors
- Promotes foreign direct investment
- Assists in obtaining various approvals and permits

## 1.5 Legal Setup: Establishing an Export-Import Business in Bangladesh

Setting up an export-import business in Bangladesh requires compliance with several legal and regulatory requirements. This section provides a step-by-step checklist for entrepreneurs.

### 1.5.1 Business Structure Selection

Before proceeding with registration, decide on the appropriate business structure:

**Options Available:**
- **Sole Proprietorship:** Individual ownership, simple setup, unlimited liability
- **Partnership:** Two or more partners, shared responsibility, partnership deed required
- **Private Limited Company:** Separate legal entity, limited liability, registered with RJSC
- **Public Limited Company:** For larger operations, can raise capital from public

**Recommendation for Export-Import:**
Private Limited Company is generally preferred for export-import businesses due to:
- Limited liability protection
- Enhanced credibility with international buyers
- Easier access to trade finance
- Better succession planning

### 1.5.2 Step-by-Step Legal Setup Checklist

#### **STEP 1: Business Name Selection and Reservation**

**Requirements:**
- ☐ Choose a unique business name
- ☐ Ensure name is not identical or similar to existing registered businesses
- ☐ Name should not contain objectionable words
- ☐ For company: Include "Limited" or "Ltd." at the end

**Procedure:**
- ☐ Visit RJSC (Registrar of Joint Stock Companies) website or office
- ☐ Search for name availability online
- ☐ Apply for name clearance (for companies)
- ☐ Pay prescribed fees
- ☐ Obtain name clearance certificate (usually within 3-5 working days)

**Documents Required:**
- ☐ Proposed names (at least 3 alternatives)
- ☐ Main objects of the business
- ☐ Applicant's identity documents

---

#### **STEP 2: Company Incorporation (If Applicable)**

**For Private Limited Company:**
- ☐ Prepare Memorandum of Association (MoA)
- ☐ Prepare Articles of Association (AoA)
- ☐ File incorporation documents with RJSC
- ☐ Pay registration fees (based on authorized capital)
- ☐ Obtain Certificate of Incorporation

**Documents Required:**
- ☐ Name clearance certificate
- ☐ MoA and AoA (signed by all subscribers)
- ☐ Declaration of compliance
- ☐ Consent of directors to act
- ☐ Address proof of registered office
- ☐ National ID copies of directors and shareholders
- ☐ Passport-size photographs of directors

**Timeline:** Typically 7-15 working days

---

#### **STEP 3: Obtaining Trade License**

**Issuing Authority:** City Corporation or Municipality or Union Parishad (depending on business location)

**Procedure:**
- ☐ Visit local City Corporation/Municipality office or apply online
- ☐ Fill in trade license application form
- ☐ Submit required documents
- ☐ Pay license fees (varies by location and business type)
- ☐ Collect trade license certificate

**Documents Required:**
- ☐ Certificate of Incorporation (for companies) or Partnership deed
- ☐ Memorandum and Articles of Association
- ☐ Holding tax/property tax payment receipt
- ☐ Rent agreement or ownership document of business premises
- ☐ National ID copy of proprietor/managing director
- ☐ Passport-size photographs
- ☐ TIN certificate

**Validity:** Usually one year, renewable annually

**Important Notes:**
- Trade license must be renewed before expiry
- Display trade license prominently at business premises
- Fees vary by city corporation and business nature

---

#### **STEP 4: Tax Identification Number (TIN) Registration**

**Issuing Authority:** National Board of Revenue (NBR)

**Procedure:**
- ☐ Visit nearest Tax Zone office or register online at NBR e-TIN portal
- ☐ Fill in TIN application form
- ☐ Submit required documents
- ☐ Receive 12-digit TIN certificate

**Documents Required:**
- ☐ National ID card copy
- ☐ Recent passport-size photograph
- ☐ Business documents (incorporation certificate, trade license)
- ☐ Address proof

**Timeline:** Immediate to 3 working days

---

#### **STEP 5: Export Registration Certificate (ERC)**

**What is ERC?**

The Export Registration Certificate is a mandatory registration issued by the Export Promotion Bureau (EPB) that authorizes a business to engage in export activities from Bangladesh. No person or entity can export goods from Bangladesh without a valid ERC.

**Legal Basis:** Import and Export Control Act, 1950

**Who Needs ERC?**
- Any individual, partnership, or company intending to export goods
- Manufacturers exporting their own products
- Trading houses engaged in export business
- Service exporters (for certain categories)

**Types of ERC:**

**a) Bonded Warehouse ERC**
- For manufacturers with bonded warehouse facilities
- Allows duty-free import of raw materials for export production

**b) General ERC**
- For general exporters without bonded facilities
- Most common type for trading companies

**Procedure to Obtain ERC:**

1. **Online Application:**
   - ☐ Visit EPB website (www.epb.gov.bd)
   - ☐ Click on "ERC Online" or "Services"
   - ☐ Create user account with valid email and mobile number
   - ☐ Fill in online ERC application form
   - ☐ Upload required documents (scanned copies)
   - ☐ Pay application fees online through bank or mobile banking

2. **Document Submission:**
   - ☐ Certificate of Incorporation (for companies) or Trade License (for proprietorship)
   - ☐ Memorandum and Articles of Association (for companies)
   - ☐ Trade License copy
   - ☐ TIN certificate
   - ☐ VAT registration certificate
   - ☐ Bank solvency certificate
   - ☐ National ID copies of proprietor/directors
   - ☐ Passport-size photographs
   - ☐ Office rent agreement or ownership documents
   - ☐ Membership certificate from Chamber of Commerce (if applicable)

3. **Verification:**
   - ☐ EPB verifies submitted documents
   - ☐ Physical inspection of business premises (if required)
   - ☐ Verification of bank account and financial standing

4. **Issuance:**
   - ☐ Receive ERC certificate online
   - ☐ Download and print ERC certificate
   - ☐ Note ERC number for all export transactions

**Fees Structure:**
- Application fees: Approximately BDT 5,000 - 10,000 (subject to change)
- Varies based on business type and category

**Validity:**
- Generally valid for unlimited period
- Must be renewed if business structure changes
- Annual declaration may be required

**ERC Number Format:** 
Example: RA-123456 (unique registration number)

**Important Conditions:**
- ERC holder must commence export within a specified period
- Must maintain proper accounts and records
- Comply with all export regulations and laws
- Realize export proceeds through banking channels
- Submit export data to EPB when required

**Renewal/Amendment:**
- ☐ Required if company name changes
- ☐ Required if business structure changes
- ☐ Change of address must be notified
- ☐ Addition/deletion of products requires amendment
- ☐ Online renewal process similar to initial application

---

#### **STEP 6: Import Registration Certificate (IRC)**

**What is IRC?**

The Import Registration Certificate is a mandatory registration issued by the Office of the Chief Controller of Imports & Exports (CCI&E) that authorizes a business to import goods into Bangladesh. No commercial import is permitted without a valid IRC.

**Legal Basis:** Import and Export Control Act, 1950

**Who Needs IRC?**
- Any business entity intending to import goods for commercial purposes
- Manufacturing companies importing raw materials
- Trading companies importing finished goods
- Individuals importing for business purposes

**Types of IRC:**

**a) Industrial IRC**
- For manufacturing industries importing raw materials and machinery
- Allows import of items related to production

**b) Commercial IRC**
- For trading companies importing finished goods
- For general import business

**c) Indenting IRC**
- For indenting houses acting as agents for foreign suppliers

**Procedure to Obtain IRC:**

1. **Online Application:**
   - ☐ Visit CCI&E website (www.ccie.gov.bd)
   - ☐ Create account on IRC online portal
   - ☐ Fill in IRC application form with business details
   - ☐ Specify category of IRC required
   - ☐ Upload scanned documents

2. **Document Submission:**
   - ☐ Certificate of Incorporation (for companies) or Trade License (for proprietorship)
   - ☐ Memorandum and Articles of Association (for companies)
   - ☐ Trade License copy (must be valid)
   - ☐ TIN certificate
   - ☐ VAT registration certificate
   - ☐ Bank solvency certificate (minimum BDT 5,00,000 recommended)
   - ☐ National ID copies of proprietor/managing director
   - ☐ Passport-size photographs
   - ☐ Office address proof (rent agreement or ownership deed)
   - ☐ Membership certificate from Chamber of Commerce (mandatory for some categories)
   - ☐ Authorization letter from board of directors (for companies)

3. **Fees Payment:**
   - ☐ Pay IRC fees through designated bank (Sonali Bank)
   - ☐ Submit payment receipt/challan with application
   - ☐ Fees: Approximately BDT 5,000 - 15,000 (varies by type)

4. **Verification Process:**
   - ☐ CCI&E verifies all submitted documents
   - ☐ Physical verification of office premises (mandatory)
   - ☐ Financial capability assessment
   - ☐ Background verification of directors/proprietors

5. **Issuance:**
   - ☐ Receive IRC certificate (physical and/or digital)
   - ☐ Note IRC registration number
   - ☐ Ensure IRC details are recorded in company records

**IRC Number Format:**
Example: IRC/DHAKA/0123456 (includes location and unique number)

**Validity:**
- **Usually valid for 5 years from date of issue**
- Must be renewed before expiry
- Late renewal may require penalties

**Renewal Process:**

**When to Renew:**
- ☐ Apply for renewal 60-90 days before expiry date
- ☐ Cannot renew after expiry (must apply as new)

**Renewal Procedure:**
- ☐ Log in to IRC online portal
- ☐ Click on renewal option
- ☐ Update any changed information
- ☐ Upload updated documents (trade license, TIN, VAT, bank solvency)
- ☐ Pay renewal fees online or through bank
- ☐ Submit application for processing

**Renewal Documents:**
- ☐ Valid trade license
- ☐ Updated TIN certificate
- ☐ Updated VAT registration
- ☐ Fresh bank solvency certificate
- ☐ Previous IRC copy
- ☐ Updated office address proof (if changed)

**Renewal Fees:** Approximately BDT 3,000 - 10,000 (subject to revision)

**Important Conditions and Obligations:**

**Mandatory Requirements:**
- IRC holder must maintain proper office premises
- Adequate financial resources to conduct import business
- Must have valid trade license throughout IRC validity
- All imports must be conducted through banking channels
- Compliance with import policy and regulations

**Record Keeping:**
- Maintain import records for at least 5 years
- Submit import data to CCI&E when required
- Keep all L/C documents properly filed

**Restrictions:**
- Some goods require special permission beyond IRC
- Must comply with product-specific import regulations
- Prohibited and restricted items cannot be imported

**Penalties for Non-Compliance:**
- IRC can be suspended or cancelled for violations
- Import without valid IRC is a punishable offense
- Misuse of IRC may lead to legal action

#### **STEP 7: VAT Registration**

**Issuing Authority:** National Board of Revenue (NBR) - VAT Division

**Who Needs VAT Registration?**
- All businesses with annual turnover exceeding BDT 30 lakh
- Export-import businesses (mandatory regardless of turnover)
- Businesses dealing in VAT-able goods

**Procedure:**
- ☐ Visit VAT online registration portal or local VAT office
- ☐ Fill in VAT registration application (Mushak 2.1)
- ☐ Submit required documents
- ☐ Pay registration fees (if applicable)
- ☐ Receive BIN (Business Identification Number)

**Documents Required:**
- ☐ Trade license copy
- ☐ TIN certificate
- ☐ Certificate of incorporation (for companies)
- ☐ National ID of proprietor/managing director
- ☐ Rent agreement or ownership proof of business premises
- ☐ Bank account details
- ☐ Photograph of proprietor/managing director

**Timeline:** 7-15 working days

**Important:**
- VAT registration provides a BIN (11-digit number)
- Required for opening L/C for imports
- Necessary for availing VAT rebates on exports
- Must file VAT returns monthly/quarterly

---

## 1.6 Export-Import Business Plan: Strategic Elements

A well-structured business plan is essential for the success of any export-import venture. It serves as a roadmap for operations and is often required when seeking financing or partnerships.

### 1.6.1 Components of an Export-Import Business Plan

#### **1. Executive Summary**

**Purpose:** Concise overview of the business plan

**Key Elements:**
- Business name and legal structure
- Mission and vision statements
- Products/services to be traded
- Target markets (countries and customer segments)
- Unique value proposition
- Funding requirements (if applicable)
- Projected financial highlights

**Length:** 1-2 pages (written last, after completing all sections)

---

#### **2. Company Description**

**Business Overview:**
- Detailed description of the business
- Legal structure and ownership
- Location and facilities
- History and background (if applicable)
- Core values and business philosophy

**Products and Services:**
- Detailed description of products to be exported/imported
- Product specifications and quality standards
- Sourcing strategy (for importers)
- Manufacturing capability (for exporters)
- Value-added services offered

**Competitive Advantages:**
- What makes your business different?
- Unique capabilities or resources
- Strategic partnerships
- Technology or innovation advantages

---

#### **3. Market Analysis**

**Industry Overview:**
- Global and regional market size
- Industry trends and growth projections
- Regulatory environment
- Key industry challenges and opportunities

**Target Market Selection:**
- Primary target countries and rationale
- Market size and growth potential
- Customer demographics and preferences
- Cultural and language considerations

**Customer Analysis:**
- Buyer personas and profiles
- Purchase behavior and decision-making process

## 1.7 Government Incentives for Exporters in Bangladesh

The Government of Bangladesh provides various incentives to promote and support export activities. Understanding these incentives can significantly improve the profitability and competitiveness of export businesses.

### 1.7.1 Cash Incentives

**Export Subsidy/Cash Incentive:**

The government provides direct cash incentives as a percentage of export value for specific sectors to enhance competitiveness.

**Eligible Sectors (Examples):**
- Readymade garments (RMG)
- Frozen food and fish
- Agricultural products
- Leather and leather goods
- Jute and jute products
- Handicrafts
- Pharmaceutical products
- ICT and software services

**Rates:** Vary by product category (typically 2%-20% of FOB value)

**How to Claim:**
- Export proceeds must be realized through banking channel
- Submit application to Bangladesh Bank through your bank
- Provide export documents (EXP form, Bill of Lading, Invoice)
- Incentive credited directly to exporter's account

---

### 1.7.2 Duty Drawback and Bonded Warehouse Facilities

**Duty Drawback Scheme:**

Exporters can claim refund of customs duties and taxes paid on imported raw materials used in exported products.

**Benefits:**
- Refund of customs duty
- Refund of regulatory duty
- Refund of VAT and supplementary duty

**Process:**
- Maintain proper records of imports and exports
- Submit duty drawback claim to Customs with evidence
- Link imported materials with exported products
- Receive refund within stipulated time

**Bonded Warehouse Facility:**

Allows manufacturers to import raw materials duty-free for export production.

**Benefits:**
- Import raw materials without paying duties upfront
- Pay duties only on goods sold domestically
- Improved cash flow and working capital
- Reduced production costs

**Eligibility:**
- Manufacturers with export commitment
- Proper warehouse facilities
- Valid ERC registration
- Minimum export performance criteria

**Types:**
- Private bonded warehouse
- Public bonded warehouse
- Special bonded warehouse

---

### 1.7.3 Export Credit and Financing Facilities

**Pre-shipment Finance:**

Banks provide working capital loans against confirmed export orders.

**Features:**
- Concessional interest rates
- Up to 80-90% of order value
- Repayable upon export realization
- Secured against export documents

**Post-shipment Finance:**

Banks provide financing after shipment until payment realization.

**Features:**
- Bridge financing for payment waiting period
- Export bill discounting/negotiation
- Lower interest rates for exporters
- Reduces cash flow pressure

**Export Development Fund (EDF):**

Bangladesh Bank operates EDF for financing import of raw materials for export.

**Benefits:**
- Low interest rate (currently around 2-3%)
- Financing for capital machinery import
- Revolving credit facility
- Reduces production cost

---

### 1.7.4 Tax Incentives

**Income Tax Exemption/Reduction:**

Certain export sectors enjoy income tax holidays or reduced rates.

**Examples:**
- Software and ICT services: Tax exemption
- RMG sector: Reduced corporate tax rates
- Export-oriented industries: Concessional tax rates

**Source Tax at Reduced Rate:**

Exporters pay source tax at lower rates on export proceeds.

**Current Rates:**
- Most export sectors: 0.5% - 1% of export value
- Varies by product category
- Adjustable against final tax liability

**VAT Exemption:**

Export goods are zero-rated for VAT purposes.

**Benefits:**
- No VAT on exported goods
- VAT paid on inputs can be refunded/adjusted
- Reduces final export price
- Improves competitiveness

---

### 1.7.5 Infrastructure and Logistical Support

**Export Processing Zones (EPZ):**

Special industrial zones with enhanced facilities for export-oriented industries.

**Benefits:**
- Duty-free import of machinery and raw materials
- Tax holidays (typically 10 years)
- Simplified regulatory procedures
- Ready infrastructure (electricity, water, telecommunications)
- Repatriation of profits allowed
- Relaxed foreign exchange regulations

**Major EPZs in Bangladesh:**
- Dhaka EPZ
- Chittagong EPZ
- Mongla EPZ
- Comilla EPZ
- Ishwardi EPZ
- Uttara EPZ

**Special Economic Zones (SEZ):**

Similar to EPZs but with larger areas and broader industrial activities.

**Benefits:**
- Infrastructure support
- One-stop service centers
- Tax incentives
- Preferential treatment

---

### 1.7.6 Market Development Support

**Trade Fair Participation:**

EPB and sector associations organize participation in international trade fairs.

**Support Provided:**
- Subsidized stall rental
- Airfare support for participants
- Marketing material development
- Buyer-seller meeting arrangements

**Trade Delegations:**

Government sponsors trade delegations to explore new markets.

**Benefits:**
- Networking with foreign buyers
- Market intelligence gathering
- Government-to-government facilitation
- B2B meeting arrangements

**Brand Promotion:**

Government initiatives for promoting "Made in Bangladesh" brand.

**Activities:**
- International advertising campaigns
- Country pavilions at major trade events
- Buyer visits to Bangladesh
- Product catalogues and promotional materials

---

### 1.7.7 Sector-Specific Incentives

**Readymade Garments (RMG):**
- Cash incentive for non-traditional markets
- Duty-free machinery import
- Bonded warehouse facilities
- Source tax at 0.5%

**Leather and Footwear:**
- Cash incentive up to 15%
- Duty drawback facilities
- Market diversification support

**Frozen Foods and Seafood:**
- Cash incentive (typically 20%)
- Quality testing support
- Cold storage facilities support

**Agricultural Products:**
- Export subsidy
- Air freight support for perishable goods
- Market access facilitation
- Quality certification support

**ICT and Software Services:**
- 100% tax exemption until 2024 (extendable)
- Repatriation facility
- Infrastructure support through Hi-Tech Parks

**Pharmaceutical Products:**
- Cash incentive for pharmaceutical exports
- Support for WHO-GMP certification
- Market access assistance

---

### 1.7.8 How to Access Government Incentives

**Step-by-Step Process:**

1. **Eligibility Verification:**
   - Check if your product/sector qualifies
   - Review specific conditions and criteria
   - Confirm minimum export requirements

2. **Documentation:**
   - Maintain proper export records
   - Ensure all regulatory compliance
   - Keep all shipping and banking documents

3. **Application Submission:**
   - Submit application through designated channel
   - Usually through Bangladesh Bank for cash incentives
   - Through sector association for trade fair support

4. **Verification:**
   - Authorities verify export authenticity
   - Document scrutiny and cross-checking
   - Bank confirmation of export proceeds realization

5. **Disbursement:**
   - Incentive credited to bank account
   - Tax exemption reflected in tax assessment
   - Duty drawback processed through customs

**Important Points:**
- Keep updated on policy changes
- Maintain accurate records and documentation
- Comply with all export regulations
- Realize export proceeds through banking channels
- Submit applications within stipulated deadlines
