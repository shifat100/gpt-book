## 8.1 Introduction

Logistics is the backbone of international trade. In the context of Bangladesh's growing export-import sector, efficient logistics management determines the competitiveness of businesses in the global market. Whether it's exporting ready-made garments to Europe or importing raw materials from Asia, the logistics system ensures that goods move from origin to destination in a timely, cost-effective, and secure manner.

This chapter explores the critical aspects of logistics and transportation management that every export-import professional must master.

## 8.2 Logistics System

### 8.2.1 Concept of Logistics System

**Logistics** refers to the detailed coordination and implementation of complex operations involving the movement of goods, services, and information from the point of origin to the point of consumption.

**International Logistics** specifically deals with:
- Planning and managing the flow of goods across international borders
- Coordinating documentation, customs clearance, and regulatory compliance
- Ensuring timely delivery while minimizing costs and risks

**Key Definition:**
> Logistics is the process of strategically managing the procurement, movement, and storage of materials, parts, and finished inventory (and the related information flows) through the organization and its marketing channels in such a way that current and future profitability are maximized through cost-effective fulfillment of orders.

### 8.2.3 Elements of a Logistics System

An effective logistics system comprises several interconnected elements:

#### **1. Transportation**

The physical movement of goods from one location to another.

**Components:**
- Mode selection (sea, air, land, multimodal)
- Route optimization
- Carrier selection
- Transit time management
- Freight cost management

**Bangladesh Context:**
- Chittagong Port handles approximately 92% of Bangladesh's international trade
- Mongla Port serves as secondary seaport
- Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport for air cargo
- Land ports: Benapole, Teknaf, Akhaura, Tamabil, etc.

#### **2. Warehousing and Storage**

Facilities for storing goods during various stages of the logistics process.

**Functions:**
- Temporary storage during transit
- Consolidation and break-bulk operations
- Value-added services (labeling, repackaging)
- Inventory management
- Protection from damage and theft

**Types in Bangladesh:**
- Private warehouses (owned by exporters/importers)
- Public warehouses (available for rent)
- Bonded warehouses (for duty-free storage)
- Container Freight Stations (CFS) at ports
- Inland Container Depots (ICD) like Dhaka ICD at Kamalapur

#### **3. Inventory Management**

The process of ordering, storing, using, and selling goods.

**Key Activities:**
- Maintaining optimal stock levels
- Demand forecasting
- Reorder point calculation
- Stock rotation (FIFO/LIFO)
- Minimizing holding costs

**Importance for Bangladesh Traders:**
- RMG sector requires just-in-time inventory of fabrics and accessories
- Seasonal demand fluctuations for agricultural exports
- Managing imported raw material inventory for manufacturing

#### **4. Packaging and Material Handling**

Protection and presentation of goods throughout the supply chain.

**Components:**
- Export-standard packaging materials
- Palletization and containerization
- Handling equipment (forklifts, cranes)
- Loading and unloading procedures
- Damage prevention measures

#### **5. Information and Communication Technology (ICT)**

Technology systems that enable tracking, documentation, and coordination.

**Systems Used:**
- Track and trace systems
- Electronic Data Interchange (EDI)
- ASYCUDA World (Bangladesh Customs automation)
- Freight management software
- GPS tracking for shipments

**Bangladesh Initiatives:**
- e-Customs system by NBR
- Port Community System at Chittagong Port
- Online documentation through Bangladesh Bank

#### **6. Order Processing**

Managing customer orders from receipt to fulfillment.

**Steps:**
- Order receipt and verification
- Order confirmation
- Documentation preparation
- Scheduling and coordination
- Shipment dispatch
- Delivery confirmation

#### **7. Documentation Management**

Handling the extensive paperwork required in international trade.

**Export Documents (Bangladesh):**
- Commercial Invoice
- Packing List
- Bill of Lading / Airway Bill
- Certificate of Origin (issued by EPB or Chambers)
- Export License (if required)
- EXP form (Export customs form)
- Insurance Certificate
- Quality/Inspection Certificate (if required)
- GSP Certificate (for preferential tariff)

**Import Documents (Bangladesh):**
- Letter of Credit (LC) or payment documents
- Commercial Invoice
- Packing List
- Bill of Lading/Airway Bill
- Insurance Certificate
- Import Permit (if required for restricted items)
- IMP form (Import customs form)
- Certificate of Origin

#### **8. Customer Service**

Ensuring buyer satisfaction through effective logistics.

**Elements:**
- Timely delivery
- Order accuracy
- Shipment tracking
- Problem resolution
- Communication and updates

### 8.2.4 Objectives of an Effective Logistics System

| **Objective** | **Description** | **Bangladesh Context** |
|---------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Cost Efficiency** | Minimize total logistics costs while maintaining service quality | Reduce transportation costs from factory to Chittagong Port; optimize container utilization |
| **Time Efficiency** | Ensure timely delivery to meet customer requirements | Quick customs clearance; minimize port congestion delays |
| **Reliability** | Consistent performance and predictable delivery | Maintain shipping schedules; reduce cargo damage rates |
| **Flexibility** | Adapt to changing market conditions and customer needs | Handle seasonal export fluctuations (RMG, jute, frozen food) |
| **Visibility** | Track shipments and provide real-time information | Use tracking systems; communicate with buyers proactively |
| **Risk Management** | Minimize losses from damage, theft, or delays | Proper insurance; secure warehousing; reliable carriers |
| **Compliance** | Adhere to all legal and regulatory requirements | Follow Bangladesh Customs regulations; meet international standards |

### 8.2.5 Challenges in Bangladesh Logistics System

Despite significant progress, Bangladesh's logistics system faces several challenges:

**Infrastructure Challenges:**
- Port congestion at Chittagong Port
- Limited container handling capacity
- Inadequate inland transportation network
- Traffic congestion in Dhaka and other major cities

**Procedural Challenges:**
- Multiple documentation requirements
- Time-consuming customs procedures
- Coordination issues between agencies
- Limited automation in some areas

**Cost Challenges:**
- High inland transportation costs
- Port charges and demurrage fees
- Container detention charges
- Warehouse rental costs

**Service Challenges:**
- Limited availability of specialized logistics services
- Shortage of trained logistics professionals
- Quality control issues in handling
- Communication gaps in the supply chain

**Government Initiatives to Address Challenges:**
- Development of Payra Sea Port (operational)
- Expansion of Chittagong Port capacity
- Introduction of ASYCUDA World system
- Establishment of more Inland Container Depots
- Simplification of customs procedures
- National Logistics Policy formulation

## 8.3 Freight Forwarders

### 8.3.1 Concept of Freight Forwarder

A **freight forwarder** is a specialized intermediary who arranges the transportation of goods on behalf of shippers (exporters or importers). They act as experts in logistics, coordinating the movement of cargo from origin to destination while handling the complex documentation and regulatory requirements.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Do not own transportation means (ships, planes, trucks)
- Act as agents or intermediaries
- Provide end-to-end logistics solutions
- Specialize in international shipping procedures
- Licensed and regulated by government authorities

**In Bangladesh:**
- Freight forwarders must be licensed by the Chief Controller of Imports & Exports (CCI&E)
- Many are members of BAFFA (Bangladesh Freight Forwarders Association)
- Operate primarily in Dhaka, Chittagong, and other port cities

### 8.3.2 Functions and Services of Freight Forwarders

Freight forwarders provide comprehensive services throughout the logistics chain:

#### **1. Transportation Arrangement**

**Services:**
- Book cargo space with shipping lines or airlines
- Negotiate freight rates on behalf of clients
- Select optimal routes and carriers
- Arrange multimodal transportation
- Coordinate pickup from factory/warehouse

**Example:** A Bangladeshi garment exporter wants to ship 20 containers to the UK. The freight forwarder books space with Maersk Line, negotiates competitive rates, and arranges truck transportation from Gazipur factory to Chittagong Port.

#### **2. Documentation Services**

**Services:**
- Prepare and process shipping documents
- Complete customs documentation
- Obtain necessary certificates and permits
- Submit documents to banks (for LC transactions)
- Ensure regulatory compliance

**Documents Handled:**
- Bill of Lading or Airway Bill
- Commercial Invoice
- Packing List
- Certificate of Origin
- Export/Import permits
- Customs declarations (EXP/IMP forms)
- Insurance certificates

#### **3. Customs Clearance**

**Services:**
- Act as customs clearing agents
- Submit documents to Bangladesh Customs (NBR)
- Calculate and pay duties and taxes (on behalf of client)
- Coordinate physical inspections
- Obtain release orders
- Handle customs queries and disputes

**Bangladesh Process:**
- Register with ASYCUDA World system
- Submit electronic customs declarations
- Coordinate with C&F agents at ports
- Handle bonded warehouse procedures

#### **4. Cargo Consolidation**

**Services:**
- Combine smaller shipments from multiple shippers
- Reduce costs through consolidated shipping
- Arrange Less than Container Load (LCL) shipments
- Deconsolidate at destination

**Benefit:** Small exporters can access container shipping rates without filling entire containers.

#### **5. Warehousing and Storage**

**Services:**
- Provide temporary storage facilities
- Arrange bonded warehouse storage
- Manage inventory at Container Freight Stations (CFS)
- Offer distribution services

#### **6. Insurance Arrangement**

**Services:**
- Arrange marine or air cargo insurance
- Process insurance claims in case of damage/loss
- Advise on appropriate coverage levels
- Coordinate with insurance companies

#### **7. Cargo Tracking and Monitoring**

**Services:**
- Provide real-time shipment tracking
- Send status updates to clients
- Monitor vessel/flight schedules
- Coordinate with carriers for updates
- Alert clients of delays or issues

#### **8. Advisory Services**

**Services:**
- Advise on packaging requirements
- Recommend optimal transportation modes
- Provide cost estimates
- Explain regulatory requirements
- Suggest trade compliance strategies

### 8.3.3 Importance of Freight Forwarders in International Trade

Freight forwarders play a crucial role in facilitating international trade, especially for businesses in developing countries like Bangladesh.

| **Importance** | **Explanation** | **Bangladesh Context** |
|----------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Expertise** | Possess specialized knowledge of shipping procedures, regulations, and documentation | Many Bangladeshi SME exporters lack in-house logistics expertise |
| **Time Saving** | Handle complex procedures, allowing businesses to focus on core activities | RMG exporters can focus on production while forwarders handle shipping |
| **Cost Reduction** | Negotiate better rates due to volume; avoid costly errors | Consolidate shipments from multiple small exporters for better rates |
| **Risk Mitigation** | Ensure proper documentation and compliance; arrange insurance | Reduce risk of shipment rejection or customs delays |
| **Network Access** | Provide access to global network of agents and carriers | Connect Bangladeshi traders with international logistics networks |
| **Problem Resolution** | Handle issues like delays, damage claims, or customs disputes | Navigate complex Bangladesh customs procedures |
| **Flexibility** | Offer various transportation options and solutions | Provide alternatives during port congestion or capacity constraints |
| **Regulatory Compliance** | Stay updated on changing regulations and ensure compliance | Navigate Bangladesh export-import regulations and international standards |

### 8.3.4 Selection Criteria for Freight Forwarders

Choosing the right freight forwarder is critical for logistics success. Consider the following criteria:

**1. Licensing and Credentials**
- Valid license from Chief Controller of Imports & Exports (Bangladesh)
- Membership in BAFFA or FIATA (International Federation of Freight Forwarders)
- Registration with Bangladesh Bank (for handling export documents)

**2. Experience and Reputation**
- Years in business
- Client testimonials and references
- Track record with similar products
- Reputation in the market

**3. Network and Coverage**
- Agent network at key destinations
- Coverage of required trade routes
- Relationships with carriers
- Presence at major ports

**4. Service Range**
- Comprehensive services (door-to-door)
- Specialized services (temperature-controlled, hazardous goods)
- Value-added services (packaging, labeling)
- Insurance arrangement capabilities

**5. Technology and Communication**
- Online tracking systems
- Electronic documentation
- Timely communication and updates
- Integration with Bangladesh customs systems

**6. Cost and Pricing**
- Competitive and transparent pricing
- No hidden charges
- Clear quotation breakdowns
- Payment terms

**7. Financial Stability**
- Financially sound organization
- Ability to handle advance payments if required
- Insurance coverage for liability

**8. Customer Service**
- Responsiveness and accessibility
- Problem-solving capability
- Dedicated account management
- Multilingual support (if needed)

### 8.3.5 Freight Forwarder vs. Customs Clearing Agent (C&F Agent)

While these terms are sometimes used interchangeably, there are distinctions:

| **Aspect** | **Freight Forwarder** | **C&F Agent** |
|------------|----------------------|---------------|
| **Primary Role** | Arrange transportation and logistics | Clear goods through customs |
| **Scope** | End-to-end logistics management | Customs clearance procedures |
| **Services** | Booking, documentation, tracking, warehousing | Customs declaration, duty payment, release |
| **Relationship** | Works with carriers, agents, warehouses | Works directly with customs authorities |
| **Licensing** | CCI&E license | Customs clearing license from NBR |
| **In Practice** | Often provide C&F services as well | May offer freight forwarding services too |

**Note:** In Bangladesh, many companies offer both freight forwarding and C&F services, providing comprehensive logistics solutions.

### 8.3.6 Major Freight Forwarders in Bangladesh

Some prominent freight forwarding companies operating in Bangladesh include:

- Expeditors Bangladesh
- Maersk Bangladesh (logistics division)
- DHL Global Forwarding
- Kuehne + Nagel
- DB Schenker
- Agility Logistics
- Various local freight forwarders and C&F agents

**Advice for Students:** When starting an export-import business, establish relationships with multiple freight forwarders to compare services and rates, and choose based on your specific shipping needs.

## 8.4 Transportation in International Trade

### 8.4.1 Concept and Importance of Transportation

**Transportation** is the physical movement of goods from the point of origin to the point of consumption. In international trade, transportation is a critical component that directly impacts cost, time, and service quality.

**Importance of Transportation:**

**1. Economic Impact**
- Constitutes 20-40% of total logistics costs
- Directly affects product pricing and competitiveness
- Influences trade volume and market access

**2. Time Efficiency**
- Determines delivery speed
- Affects inventory management
- Impacts customer satisfaction

**3. Market Access**
- Enables access to distant markets
- Facilitates global trade
- Connects producers with consumers worldwide

**4. Trade Facilitation**
- Moves raw materials to manufacturers
- Distributes finished goods to markets
- Supports just-in-time manufacturing

**5. National Development**
- Supports export-led growth (crucial for Bangladesh)
- Creates employment in logistics sector
- Generates foreign exchange earnings

**Bangladesh Context:**

Bangladesh's export success, particularly in the ready-made garment sector, depends heavily on efficient transportation. The country's strategic location in South Asia provides access to major shipping routes connecting Asia, Europe, and the Americas.

**Key Statistics:**
- Over 90% of Bangladesh's international trade (by volume) moves by sea
- Chittagong Port handles approximately 3 million TEUs (Twenty-foot Equivalent Units) annually
- Air cargo exports primarily include high-value garments, electronics, and perishables
- Land trade with India through border ports supports regional commerce

### 8.4.2 Modes of Transportation

There are five primary modes of transportation in international trade, each with distinct characteristics, advantages, and limitations.

#### **MODE 1: Sea/Ocean Transportation**

The most widely used mode for international cargo movement, especially for heavy and bulky goods.

**Types of Vessels:**

**a) Container Ships**
- Carry standardized containers (20ft and 40ft)
- Most common for general cargo
- Efficient loading/unloading using cranes
- Used for Bangladesh RMG exports

**b) Bulk Carriers**
- Transport loose cargo (grain, coal, ore)
- Used for Bangladeshi agricultural exports
- No packaging required

**c) Tankers**
- Carry liquid cargo (oil, chemicals)
- Specialized construction
- Limited relevance for typical Bangladesh exports

**d) Roll-on/Roll-off (RoRo) Ships**
- Vehicles drive on and off
- Used for automobile imports to Bangladesh

**e) General Cargo Ships**
- Handle break-bulk cargo
- Less common than container ships
- Used for oversized equipment

**Advantages:**

| **Advantage** | **Description** |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Cost-Effective** | Lowest per-unit transportation cost for large volumes |
| **High Capacity** | Can carry massive quantities of cargo |
| **Suitable for Heavy Goods** | Ideal for machinery, raw materials, bulky items |
| **Global Coverage** | Connects all major ports worldwide |
| **Environmental** | Lower carbon emissions per ton-kilometer compared to air |

**Disadvantages:**

| **Disadvantage** | **Description** |
|------------------|-----------------|
| **Slow** | Transit time of 2-6 weeks for typical routes |
| **Port Dependency** | Requires developed port infrastructure |
| **Weather Dependent** | Subject to delays due to storms, rough seas |
| **Less Frequent** | Fewer sailing schedules compared to other modes |
| **Risk of Damage** | Longer exposure to humidity, movement |

**Bangladesh Sea Transportation:**

**Major Ports:**
- **Chittagong Port:** Main seaport handling 92% of trade
- **Mongla Port:** Secondary port in Khulna region
- **Payra Sea Port:** Newly developed deep-sea port

**Key Routes:**
- Bangladesh to Europe (via Suez Canal)
- Bangladesh to North America (via Suez or Cape of Good Hope)
- Bangladesh to East Asia (direct routes)
- Bangladesh to Middle East (shorter routes)

**Common Container Types:**
- 20ft containers (TEU) for standard cargo
- 40ft containers (FEU) for RMG shipments
- 40ft high-cube containers for voluminous goods
- Refrigerated containers (reefers) for frozen food exports

**Shipping Lines Operating in Bangladesh:**
- Maersk Line
- MSC (Mediterranean Shipping Company)
- CMA CGM
- Hapag-Lloyd
- COSCO
- Various feeder services

**Typical Transit Times from Chittagong:**
- To European ports: 18-25 days
- To US East Coast: 25-35 days
- To US West Coast: 25-30 days (via Suez)
- To Middle East: 10-15 days
- To East Asia: 7-14 days

#### **MODE 2: Air Transportation**

Air cargo services provide the fastest international transportation option.

**Types of Air Services:**

**a) Passenger Aircraft (Belly Cargo)**
- Cargo carried in holds of passenger planes
- Limited capacity
- Frequent schedules

**b) Dedicated Cargo Aircraft**
- Entirely for freight
- Larger capacity
- More flexible scheduling

**c) Charter Services**
- Entire aircraft chartered for specific shipment
- Used for urgent or oversized cargo
- Expensive option

**Advantages:**

| **Advantage** | **Description** |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Speed** | Transit time of 1-7 days for most international routes |
| **Reliability** | Fixed schedules with high punctuality |
| **Less Packaging** | Shorter transit reduces damage risk |
| **Safety** | Lower risk of theft and damage |
| **Global Reach** | Access to remote locations quickly |
| **Reduced Inventory Costs** | Fast delivery enables just-in-time delivery |

**Disadvantages:**

| **Disadvantage** | **Description** |
|------------------|-----------------|
| **Expensive** | 5-10 times higher cost than sea freight |
| **Limited Capacity** | Cannot handle very large or heavy shipments |
| **Weight Restrictions** | Airlines impose strict weight and size limits |
| **Environmental Impact** | Higher carbon footprint per ton-kilometer |
| **Weather Sensitivity** | Flights may be delayed or cancelled |

**Suitable Cargo for Air Transportation:**

- High-value goods (electronics, jewelry)
- Perishable items (fresh food, flowers)
- Urgent shipments (samples, documents)
- Lightweight, high-value garments (fashion items with tight deadlines)
- Medical supplies and pharmaceuticals
- Time-sensitive components

**Bangladesh Air Cargo:**

**Major Airport:**
- **Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport, Dhaka:** Main international cargo hub

**Cargo Airlines Operating:**
- Emirates SkyCargo
- Qatar Airways Cargo
- Turkish Cargo
- Singapore Airlines Cargo
- FedEx Express
- DHL Aviation

**Key Exports by Air from Bangladesh:**
- Ready-made garments (urgent orders, samples)
- Frozen fish and seafood
- Fresh vegetables
- Pharmaceutical products
- Electronics and accessories

**Typical Transit Times from Dhaka:**
- To Europe: 1-2 days
- To North America: 2-3 days
- To Middle East: 1-2 days
- To East Asia: 1-2 days

**Air Freight Charges:**

Air freight is typically charged by:
- **Actual Weight** or **Volumetric Weight**, whichever is higher
- Volumetric Weight = (Length × Width × Height in cm) ÷ 6000
- Additional charges: fuel surcharge, security fees, handling fees

#### **MODE 3: Road Transportation**

Road transport is essential for inland movement and cross-border trade with neighboring countries.

**Types of Road Vehicles:**

**a) Trucks**
- Small trucks (1-5 tons capacity)
- Medium trucks (5-15 tons)
- Heavy trucks (15-30 tons)
- Container trucks (carry 20ft/40ft containers)

**b) Trailer Trucks**
- Articulated vehicles with detachable trailers
- Higher capacity (30-40 tons)
- Common for long-distance inland transport

**c) Covered Vans**
- Enclosed body for weather protection
- Suitable for finished goods
- Common for RMG factory-to-port transport

**Advantages:**

| **Advantage** | **Description** |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Door-to-Door Service** | Direct pickup and delivery possible |
| **Flexibility** | Can reach any location with road access |
| **Frequency** | Multiple trips possible per day |
| **Faster for Short Distances** | Quick delivery within 500 km radius |
| **Lower Packaging Cost** | Less handling reduces packaging needs |
| **Suitable for Small Shipments** | Economic for less than container loads |

**Disadvantages:**

| **Disadvantage** | **Description** |
|------------------|-----------------|
| **Distance Limitations** | Uneconomical for very long distances |
| **Traffic Congestion** | Delays in urban areas and highways |
| **Weather Dependency** | Floods, landslides affect operations |
| **Road Quality Issues** | Poor roads increase costs and transit time |
| **Higher Risk of Accidents** | More prone to accidents than rail or sea |

**Bangladesh Road Transportation:**

**Inland Transport:**
- Factory to Chittagong Port (from Dhaka, Gazipur, Narayanganj)
- Port to inland container depots
- Distribution within Bangladesh

**Cross-Border Road Transport:**

**Major Land Ports:**
- **Benapole:** India border (largest land port)
- **Teknaf:** Myanmar border
- **Akhaura:** India border
- **Tamabil:** India border (Meghalaya)
- **Burimari:** India border (West Bengal/Assam)
- **Banglabandha:** India border (northernmost point)

**Trade Routes:**
- Bangladesh-India: Bilateral trade via land ports
- Bangladesh-Nepal: Via India (transit agreement)
- Bangladesh-Bhutan: Via India (transit agreement)
- Regional connectivity through BBIN (Bangladesh-Bhutan-India-Nepal) initiative

**Challenges:**
- Traffic congestion on Dhaka-Chittagong highway
- Long waiting times at border crossings
- Limited road quality in some regions
- Overloading issues
- Multiple checkpoints and toll plazas

#### **MODE 4: Rail Transportation**

Rail transport offers a cost-effective option for heavy and bulk cargo over long distances.

**Types of Rail Services:**

**a) General Freight Trains**
- Mixed cargo in wagons
- Slower but cheaper

**b) Container Trains**
- Carry standardized containers
- Direct rail connection to ports
- Faster and more efficient

**c) Bulk Cargo Trains**
- Specialized wagons for bulk goods
- Used for coal, grain, cement

**Advantages:**

| **Advantage** | **Description** |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Cost-Effective** | Lower cost than road for long distances |
| **High Capacity** | Can carry large volumes in single trip |
| **Energy Efficient** | Lower fuel consumption per ton-kilometer |
| **Reliable** | Less affected by traffic congestion |
| **Environmentally Friendly** | Lower carbon emissions than trucks |
| **Suitable for Heavy Cargo** | Can handle very heavy shipments |

**Disadvantages:**

| **Disadvantage** | **Description** |
|------------------|-----------------|
| **Limited Network** | Restricted to rail line locations |
| **Inflexible** | Cannot provide door-to-door service |
| **Slow** | Multiple stops and shunting increase time |
| **Infrastructure Dependent** | Requires developed rail infrastructure |
| **Damage Risk** | Frequent handling and coupling/uncoupling |

**Bangladesh Rail Transportation:**

**Current Status:**
- Bangladesh Railway operates limited freight services
- Chittagong Port has rail connectivity
- Rail share in cargo transport is very low (less than 5%)

**Limitations:**
- Outdated rail infrastructure
- Limited coverage of industrial areas
- Slow service and frequent delays
- Preference for road transport

**Government Initiatives:**
- Railway expansion projects
- Modernization of existing lines
- Plans for dedicated freight corridors

**Potential for Cross-Border Rail:**
- India-Bangladesh rail links exist
- Potential for enhanced regional connectivity
- Container train services under discussion

#### **MODE 5: Multimodal/Intermodal Transportation**

Multimodal transport involves using two or more modes of transportation under a single contract and transport document.

**Concept:**

**Multimodal Transport:** A single transport operator (Multimodal Transport Operator - MTO) takes responsibility for the entire journey using multiple modes.

**Intermodal Transport:** Different transport operators handle different legs, using multiple modes with standardized containers.

**Common Combinations:**

**a) Sea-Road (Most Common)**
- Container shipped by sea
- Truck delivery from port to final destination
- Used for most Bangladesh exports

**b) Air-Road**
- Air freight to airport
- Road delivery to final destination
- Common for urgent shipments

**c) Rail-Sea**
- Rail transport to port
- Sea freight for international leg
- Emerging in Bangladesh

**d) Road-Sea-Road**
- Truck from factory to origin port
- Sea freight internationally
- Truck from destination port to buyer's location
- Standard process for containerized exports

**Advantages:**

| **Advantage** | **Description** |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Single Responsibility** | One operator coordinates entire journey |
| **One Document** | Single transport document for all modes |
| **Optimized Routing** | Best combination of speed and cost |
| **Reduced Handling** | Containerization minimizes cargo handling |
| **Cost Efficiency** | Utilizes strengths of each mode |
| **Simplified Management** | Easier for shippers to manage |

**Disadvantages:**

| **Disadvantage** | **Description** |
|------------------|-----------------|
| **Complexity** | Requires sophisticated coordination |
| **Infrastructure Needs** | Requires compatible infrastructure at transfer points |
| **Liability Issues** | Complex in case of damage/loss at transfer points |
| **Higher Coordination Costs** | Management and coordination expenses |

**Documents:**

- **Multimodal Transport Document (MTD)** or **Combined Transport Bill of Lading (CTBL)**
- Covers entire journey from origin to destination
- Issued by Multimodal Transport Operator (MTO)

**Bangladesh Example:**

A typical export from Bangladesh to Europe involves:

1. **Road:** Garment factory in Gazipur → Chittagong Port (truck)
2. **Sea:** Chittagong Port → Rotterdam Port (container ship)
3. **Road/Rail:** Rotterdam Port → Buyer's warehouse in Germany (truck/train)

The freight forwarder acts as MTO and issues a multimodal transport document covering all three legs.

### 8.4.3 Comparison of Transportation Modes

| **Factor** | **Sea** | **Air** | **Road** | **Rail** | **Multimodal** |
|------------|---------|---------|----------|----------|----------------|
| **Speed** | Slow (15-40 days) | Very Fast (1-7 days) | Medium (1-3 days inland) | Slow-Medium | Varies |
| **Cost** | Very Low | Very High | Medium | Low | Medium |
| **Capacity** | Very High | Low | Medium | High | High |
| **Accessibility** | Port cities only | Major airports | Everywhere with roads | Limited to rail lines | Comprehensive |
| **Reliability** | Medium (weather delays) | High | Medium (traffic) | Medium | High |
| **Flexibility** | Low | Medium | Very High | Low | High |
| **Suitability** | Bulk, heavy goods | High-value, urgent | General cargo | Bulk, heavy | Varies |
| **Documentation** | Complex | Simple | Simple | Simple | Comprehensive |
| **Environmental Impact** | Low per unit | High per unit | Medium | Low | Varies |
| **Bangladesh Usage** | 90%+ of trade | <5% by value | Primary inland | Minimal | Growing |

### 8.4.4 Selection Criteria for Transportation Mode

Choosing the right transportation mode requires careful analysis of multiple factors:

#### **1. Nature of Goods**

**Considerations:**
- Perishability (air for fresh items)
- Value (air for high-value, sea for low-value)
- Weight and volume (sea for heavy/bulky)
- Fragility (less handling preferred)
- Hazardous nature (special requirements)

**Bangladesh Examples:**
- **RMG Exports:** Primarily sea (bulk orders) + air (urgent/samples)
- **Frozen Seafood:** Air for premium markets, sea with reefers for others
- **Jute:** Sea (bulk, low value per unit)
- **Leather Goods:** Combination based on urgency and value

#### **2. Cost Considerations**

**Total Cost Analysis:**
- Transportation freight charges
- Insurance costs
- Packaging expenses
- Warehousing and storage
- Customs and handling charges
- Inventory holding costs

**Rule of Thumb:**
- If transportation cost < 5% of product value → consider air
- If product highly time-sensitive → air despite high cost
- If margins are tight → sea to minimize costs

**Bangladesh Context:**
- RMG operates on thin margins (3-7%), so sea freight is preferred
- Express shipments of garment samples by air justified by potential order value

#### **3. Transit Time Requirements**

**Considerations:**
- Buyer's deadline
- Product shelf life
- Seasonal demand factors
- Competitive delivery standards
- Inventory management strategy

**Examples:**
- Fashion garments for fast-fashion brands: air or express sea
- Basic garments with stable demand: regular sea freight
- Machinery parts (just-in-time manufacturing): air

#### **4. Distance**

**General Guidelines:**
- < 500 km: Road transport optimal
- 500-2000 km: Road or rail competitive
- > 2000 km (international): Sea or air depending on urgency

**Bangladesh Context:**
- Within Bangladesh: Road transport dominates
- To India: Land ports for certain goods, sea for others
- To Europe/Americas: Sea predominantly
- To Middle East: Sea (shorter routes)

#### **5. Frequency and Availability**

**Considerations:**
- Shipping schedules from Chittagong Port
- Flight frequencies from Dhaka Airport
- Carrier options and competition
- Seasonal capacity constraints

**Bangladesh Challenges:**
- Limited direct shipping lines to smaller destinations
- Seasonal capacity shortages during peak export periods (pre-Christmas)
- Need to book space well in advance

#### **6. Reliability and Safety**

**Considerations:**
- On-time delivery track record
- Damage and loss rates
- Insurance costs and coverage
- Political and security factors
- Weather impact

**Bangladesh Concerns:**
- Port congestion leading to delays
- Monsoon impact on road transport
- Container damage during handling

#### **7. Packaging Requirements**

**Considerations:**
- Export packaging costs
- Suitability of packaging for mode
- Protection level needed
- Space utilization efficiency

**Example:**
- Sea freight requires more robust packaging (moisture, movement)
- Air freight allows lighter packaging
- Impact on overall cost structure

#### **8. Customs and Regulatory Factors**

**Considerations:**
- Documentation requirements
- Customs clearance procedures
- Import/export regulations at destination
- Product-specific certifications

**Bangladesh Context:**
- Some countries require specific certificates for Bangladeshi products
- Preference certifications (GSP, EBA) affect competitiveness
- Compliance with buyer country standards

#### **9. Market Factors**

**Considerations:**
- Customer expectations and requirements
- Competitor delivery standards
- Market positioning (premium vs. value)
- Payment terms (impact on cash flow)

**Strategic Decisions:**
- Investing in faster delivery as competitive advantage
- Balancing cost and service for market positioning

#### **10. Environmental Considerations**

**Growing Importance:**
- Buyer requirements for sustainable logistics
- Carbon footprint reporting
- Corporate social responsibility commitments

**Options:**
- Sea freight: lowest emissions per ton-km
- Rail: second-lowest
- Road: medium emissions
- Air: highest emissions

**Bangladesh Green Logistics:**
- Increasing buyer pressure for sustainable practices
- Potential for green logistics certification
- Long-term cost benefits

### 8.4.6 Transportation Documents

Each mode of transportation requires specific documents:

#### **Sea Transportation Documents**

**Bill of Lading (B/L)**

The most important document in sea transportation.

**Types:**
- **Ocean Bill of Lading:** For port-to-port shipment
- **Through Bill of Lading:** For multimodal transport
- **Clean Bill of Lading:** No remarks on cargo condition
- **Claused Bill of Lading:** Notes damage or defects

**Functions:**
- Receipt of goods by carrier
- Contract of carriage
- Document of title (negotiable)

**Key Information:**
- Shipper and consignee details
- Port of loading and discharge
- Description of goods
- Freight charges
- Number of containers/packages

**Bangladesh Context:**
- Issued by shipping lines or their agents at Chittagong Port
- Original B/L required for customs clearance at destination
- Multiple originals issued (typically 3)
- Surrender B/L gaining popularity for faster release

**Container Seal and Stuffing Certificate**
- Certifies container loading
- Records seal number
- Verifies cargo quantity

#### **Air Transportation Documents**

**Air Waybill (AWB)**

**Characteristics:**
- Non-negotiable (unlike B/L)
- Serves as receipt and contract
- Numbered document (airline-specific)

**Types:**
- **Master Air Waybill (MAWB):** Issued by airline
- **House Air Waybill (HAWB):** Issued by freight forwarder

**Information Included:**
- Shipper and consignee
- Airports (origin and destination)
- Flight details
- Weight and dimensions
- Freight charges

#### **Road Transportation Documents**

**Truck Receipt / Lorry Receipt (LR)**
- Issued by truck operator
- Acts as receipt for goods
- Used for domestic transport in Bangladesh

**For Cross-Border Road Transport:**
- Customs transit documents
- Vehicle registration papers
- Driver permits
- Cargo manifest

#### **Rail Transportation Documents**

**Railway Receipt (RR)**
- Issued by railway authorities
- Non-negotiable
- Covers rail journey

**Bangladesh:** Limited usage due to minimal rail freight

#### **Multimodal Transportation Documents**

**Multimodal Transport Document (MTD) / Combined Transport Bill of Lading**
- Single document for entire journey
- Issued by Multimodal Transport Operator (MTO)
- Can be negotiable or non-negotiable
- Covers all modes used

## 8.5 Packaging and Labeling for Export

Proper packaging and labeling are critical for protecting goods during international transit and ensuring compliance with destination country regulations.

### 8.5.1 Importance of Export Packaging

Export packaging serves multiple critical functions:

#### **1. Protection During Transit**

**Physical Protection:**
- Safeguards against impact and vibration
- Protects from compression during stacking
- Prevents damage from handling equipment

**Environmental Protection:**
- Moisture and humidity (especially sea freight)
- Temperature fluctuations
- Dust and contamination
- Exposure to sunlight

**Security:**
- Deters theft and pilferage
- Enables tamper-evident sealing
- Maintains cargo integrity

#### **2. Compliance with Regulations**

**International Standards:**
- ISPM 15 (wood packaging standards)
- IATA regulations (air cargo)
- IMO regulations (sea cargo)
- Destination country requirements

**Product-Specific Requirements:**
- Food products (food-grade packaging)
- Pharmaceuticals (temperature-controlled)
- Hazardous materials (special containers)
- Fragile items (cushioning requirements)

#### **3. Cost Optimization**

**Efficient Space Utilization:**
- Maximizes container loading
- Reduces wasted space
- Optimizes freight charges

**Damage Reduction:**
- Prevents product loss
- Avoids insurance claims
- Maintains product quality

#### **4. Marketing and Brand Image**

**Professional Presentation:**
- Reflects quality standards
- Builds buyer confidence
- Supports brand positioning

**Information Communication:**
- Product details and specifications
- Handling instructions
- Origin and destination information

#### **5. Handling Facilitation**

**Ease of Movement:**
- Standardized dimensions for equipment
- Proper marking for identification
- Weight distribution for safe handling

### 8.5.2 Levels of Export Packaging

Export packaging typically involves three levels:

#### **Level 1: Primary Packaging (Consumer Packaging)**

**Purpose:** Contains individual product units

**Examples:**
- Plastic bags for individual garments
- Boxes for shoes
- Bottles for liquids
- Blister packs for small items

**Bangladesh RMG Context:**
- Individual polybags for each garment piece
- Size labels, price tags attached
- Buyer-specific packaging requirements
- Eco-friendly packaging increasingly demanded

**Considerations:**
- Must meet destination retail requirements
- Should be attractive for display
- Needs appropriate labeling
- Environmental sustainability

#### **Level 2: Secondary Packaging (Inner Packaging)**

**Purpose:** Groups primary packages for handling and distribution

**Examples:**
- Cartons containing multiple pieces
- Shrink-wrapped bundles
- Display-ready packaging

**Bangladesh RMG Context:**
- Cartons containing specific quantities per size/color
- Typically 12-48 pieces per carton
- Dimensions optimized for container loading
- Strong corrugated boxes used

**Considerations:**
- Should facilitate counting and distribution
- Enables warehouse storage
- Protects primary packaging
- Allows partial unpacking

#### **Level 3: Tertiary Packaging (Outer/Export Packaging)**

**Purpose:** Facilitates transportation and protects during long-distance transit

**Examples:**
- Wooden crates
- Pallets
- Shrink-wrapped pallets
- Export cartons

**Bangladesh Export Packaging:**
- Strong corrugated cartons (usually tertiary level for RMG)
- Palletization for certain products
- Strapping and wrapping
- Container stuffing

**Considerations:**
- Must withstand international transit
- Compliance with container dimensions
- Moisture and damage protection
- Handling equipment compatibility

### 8.5.3 Types of Export Packaging Materials

| **Material** | **Characteristics** | **Uses** | **Bangladesh Application** |
|--------------|---------------------|----------|----------------------------|
| **Corrugated Cartons** | Lightweight, strong, recyclable, cost-effective | General cargo, garments, electronics | Primary export packaging for RMG |
| **Wooden Crates** | Very strong, reusable, heavy | Heavy machinery, equipment | Machinery exports, industrial goods |
| **Wooden Pallets** | Enables mechanical handling, standardized sizes | Base for stacking cartons | Container loading optimization |

### 8.5.4 Packaging Standards and Requirements

#### **ISPM 15 (International Standards for Phytosanitary Measures No. 15)**

**Purpose:** Prevents spread of plant pests and diseases through wood packaging

**Requirements:**
- All wood packaging (pallets, crates, dunnage) must be:
  - Heat-treated (HT) or fumigated with methyl bromide (MB)
  - Properly marked with ISPM 15 stamp
  - Made from debarked wood

**Marking Format:**
```
[Country Code]-[Producer Code]-[Treatment Code]

Example: BD-123-HT
BD = Bangladesh
123 = Producer registration number
HT = Heat Treatment
```

**Bangladesh Context:**
- Exporters must use ISPM 15 certified wood packaging for most destinations
- Treatment facilities available in Bangladesh
- Required for USA, EU, Canada, Australia, and many other countries
- Non-compliance results in shipment rejection or additional treatment costs

#### **Container Loading Requirements**

**Standard Container Specifications:**

**20ft Container (TEU):**
- Internal dimensions: 5.9m (L) × 2.35m (W) × 2.39m (H)
- Capacity: approximately 28-30 cubic meters
- Typical load: 10-12 tons (varies by shipping line)

**40ft Container (FEU):**
- Internal dimensions: 12m (L) × 2.35m (W) × 2.39m (H)
- Capacity: approximately 58-60 cubic meters
- Typical load: 20-22 tons

**40ft High Cube Container:**
- Internal dimensions: 12m (L) × 2.35m (W) × 2.69m (H)
- Capacity: approximately 68-70 cubic meters
- Commonly used for RMG (voluminous but lightweight)

**Loading Principles:**
- Distribute weight evenly
- Heavy items at bottom
- Secure cargo to prevent shifting
- Use dunnage (wood/cardboard) for stability
- Leave no empty spaces (stuffing efficiency)
- Document container stuffing (photos recommended)

#### **Destination-Specific Requirements**

Different countries have varying packaging requirements:

**European Union:**
- ISPM 15 compliance for wood
- Plastic packaging recyclability
- Labeling in local languages (some products)
- Environmental packaging directives

**United States:**
- ISPM 15 compliance
- Food contact materials approval
- Bioterrorism Act compliance (food)
- Product-specific FDA requirements

**Middle East:**
- Cultural sensitivity in imagery
- Arabic labeling for certain products
- Halal certification display
- Country of origin marking

**Australia:**
- Strict biosecurity measures
- ISPM 15 compliance
- Quarantine inspection requirements
- Additional treatment if non-compliant

### 8.5.5 Labeling Requirements for Export

Proper labeling is essential for identification, handling, and compliance.

#### **Types of Export Labels**

**1. Shipping Marks (Main Marks)**

**Purpose:** Primary identification of packages

**Information Included:**
- Consignee name or code
- Destination (port/city/country)
- Order number or reference
- Package number (e.g., 1/100, 2/100)
- Country of origin

**Example:**
```
ABC RETAILERS INC.
HAMBURG, GERMANY
ORDER NO: 12345
MADE IN BANGLADESH
CARTON 1/150
```

**Bangladesh RMG Practice:**
- Usually printed on shipping carton
- Buyer-specific marking requirements
- May include style number and color
- Country of origin prominently displayed

**2. Handling Marks (Care Labels)**

**Purpose:** Indicate special handling requirements

**Standard Pictograms (ISO 780):**
- Fragile (broken glass symbol)
- Keep dry (umbrella symbol)
- This side up (arrows)
- Stack limit (stacked boxes with number)
- Do not use hooks (crossed hook symbol)
- Center of gravity (balance symbol)
- Temperature range (thermometer)

**Bangladesh Context:**
- Essential for electronics, machinery, fragile items
- Less common for standard RMG cartons
- Important for mixed cargo shipments

**3. Warning Labels**

**Purpose:** Safety and regulatory compliance

**Examples:**
- Hazardous materials classification
- Flammable/Explosive warnings
- Toxic substance indicators
- Lithium battery warnings (air cargo)

**Bangladesh Application:**
- Chemical exports
- Battery/electronics shipments
- Pharmaceutical products

**4. Information Labels (Product Details)**

**Purpose:** Provide product and regulatory information

**Contents:**
- Product name and description
- Quantity and net weight
- Manufacturer details
- Country of origin
- Certifications (ISO, CE, etc.)
- Barcode (if required)

**5. Regulatory Labels**

**Purpose:** Compliance with destination country regulations

**Examples:**
- CE marking (European Union)
- FCC marking (USA electronics)
- Energy efficiency labels
- Fiber content labels (textiles)
- Care instruction labels (garments)

**Bangladesh RMG Labels:**
- Fiber content (e.g., "100% Cotton")
- Care instructions with symbols
- Country of origin
- Brand labels
- Size labels
- Compliance certifications (Oeko-Tex, GOTS, etc.)

#### **Label Placement and Quality**

**Best Practices:**

**Visibility:**
- Place on at least two sides of package
- Use contrasting colors (black on white/yellow)
- Adequate size for easy reading
- Weather-resistant materials

**Durability:**
- Water-resistant labels
- Strong adhesive
- Protected from abrasion
- UV-resistant ink

**Accuracy:**
- Verify all information before printing
- Consistency across all packages
- Clear and legible fonts
- Correct language (if required)

**Position:**
- Not on package seams or closure areas
- Avoid covering existing labels
- Accessible for scanning/inspection
- Protected during handling

#### **Country of Origin Marking**

**Importance:**
- Legal requirement in many countries
- Determines duty rates
- Preference eligibility (GSP, FTA)
- Consumer information

**Bangladesh Marking:**
- "Made in Bangladesh" clearly displayed
- On product labels and shipping cartons
- Permanent marking for certain products
- Compliance with buyer requirements

**Rules:**
- Country where substantial transformation occurred
- In some cases, must be in English
- Non-removable for certain products
- Penalties for false marking

### 8.5.6 Packaging Best Practices for Bangladesh Exporters

**For Ready-Made Garment Exporters:**

1. **Use high-quality polybags**
   - Thickness appropriate for transit
   - Eco-friendly options (biodegradable) if buyer requires

   - Not exceeding weight limit (usually 15-20 kg per carton)

4. **Labeling**
   - Clear shipping marks
   - Size stickers and ratio labels
   - Style/PO number visible
   - Made in Bangladesh marking

5. **Container stuffing**
   - Maximize space utilization
   - Even weight distribution
   - Secure stacking
   - Take loading photos (evidence)

**For Other Products:**

**Frozen Food/Seafood:**
- Insulated/refrigerated packaging
- Moisture-proof materials
- Temperature monitoring labels
- Air-tight sealing

**Jute Products:**
- Moisture protection (plastic lining)
- Compression for space efficiency
- Proper ventilation if required
- Fumigation certificate if needed

**Leather Products:**
- Acid-free tissue paper wrapping
- Individual boxes for shoes/bags
- Moisture absorbers

**Handicrafts:**
- Individual wrapping with cushioning
- Fragile markings
- Cultural packaging presentation
- Damage-proof outer cartons

## 8.6 Chapter Summary

**Logistics System:**
- Logistics involves the integrated management of transportation, warehousing, inventory, packaging, and information
- The logistics process includes order processing, packaging, warehousing, customs clearance, transportation, and delivery
- Key elements: transportation, warehousing, inventory management, ICT, documentation, and customer service
- Bangladesh faces challenges in port infrastructure, customs procedures, and inland connectivity

**Freight Forwarders:**
- Act as intermediaries coordinating cargo movement
- Provide services including transportation booking, documentation, customs clearance, warehousing, and insurance
- Licensed by Chief Controller of Imports & Exports in Bangladesh
- Essential for SMEs lacking in-house logistics expertise
- Selection criteria include licensing, experience, network, services, technology, and cost

**Transportation Modes:**
- **Sea transport:** Most common (90%+ of Bangladesh trade), cost-effective for bulk/heavy cargo, slow
- **Air transport:** Fastest but expensive, suitable for urgent/high-value goods, limited capacity
- **Road transport:** Flexible door-to-door service, essential for inland movement and cross-border trade
- **Rail transport:** Limited in Bangladesh, cost-effective for bulk cargo over long distances
- **Multimodal transport:** Combines multiple modes under single operator, optimizes cost and service

**Mode Selection Criteria:**
- Nature of goods, cost, transit time requirements, distance, frequency, reliability, packaging needs, regulations, market factors, environmental impact
- Decision requires analyzing product characteristics, buyer requirements, and total landed cost

**Packaging and Labeling:**
- Three levels: primary (consumer), secondary (handling), tertiary (export/transit)
- Functions: protection, compliance, cost optimization, marketing, handling facilitation
- ISPM 15 compliance required for wood packaging to most destinations
- Labels include shipping marks, handling marks, warnings, product information, and regulatory labels
- Country of origin marking legally required
- Environmental sustainability increasingly important

**Bangladesh Context:**
- Chittagong Port dominates international trade
- RMG sector requires specialized packaging (polybags, cartons)
- ISPM 15 treatment facilities available
- Growing emphasis on eco-friendly packaging
- Infrastructure development ongoing (Payra Port, Dhaka ICD expansion)

## 8.7 Key Terms

- **Container Freight Station (CFS):** Facility for consolidation/deconsolidation of cargo

- **Volumetric Weight:** Calculated weight based on package dimensions
