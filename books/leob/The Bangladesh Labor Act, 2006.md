# Chapter 6: The Bangladesh Labor Act, 2006

## 1. Important Definitions

### **Factory**

* A *factory* refers to any premises where:

  * **10 or more workers** are working with the aid of power; or
  * **20 or more workers** are working without the aid of power;
  * And where a manufacturing process is carried out.

### **Worker**

A *worker* means any person employed directly or through a contractor:

* To perform skilled, unskilled, manual, technical, trade, operational, or clerical work.
* Excludes members of the employer's family and managerial/administrative staff.

### **Employer**

An *employer* includes:

* The owner of an establishment.
* A manager, agent, or person responsible for its supervision or control.
* Any individual who employs workers and pays wages.

### **Commercial Establishment**

* Establishments engaged in **commercial, trading, banking, insurance, or administrative activities**.
* Includes hotels, restaurants, shops, and transport services.

### **Industrial Establishment**

* Includes factories, workshops, manufacturing units, and any place where **industry-related activities** occur.

---

## 2. Employment Conditions

### **Employment and Conditions of Services**

* Employers must issue an **Appointment Letter** and **Identity Card**.
* Service book must be maintained with employment history.
* Every establishment must create and maintain **service rules** with approval from authorities.
* Workers must be paid wages within **7 working days** of wage period.

### **Employment of Adolescent**

* Adolescent refers to persons aged **14 to below 18 years**.
* A *fitness certificate* from a registered doctor is mandatory.
* Adolescents cannot be employed in hazardous work.
* Working hours must not exceed prescribed limits.

---

## 3. Health & Safety Measures

### **Hygiene Provisions**

* Cleanliness of factory premises.
* Adequate ventilation and lighting.
* Safe drinking water must be supplied.
* Separate washrooms for male and female workers.

### **Safety Measures**

* Fencing of machinery.
* Emergency exits and fire safety equipment.
* Protective gear for hazardous work.
* Precautions for explosive or inflammable materials.

### **Welfare Measures**

* First-aid boxes and trained personnel.
* Canteen facilities for factories with more than 250 workers.
* Restrooms and lunch rooms.
* Childcare rooms for establishments employing **40+ female workers**.

---

## 4. Working Hours, Leave & Holidays

### **Working Hours**

* Daily working hour: **8 hours**.
* Weekly working hour: **48 hours**.
* Maximum overtime allowed: up to **60 hours per week**, subject to conditions.

### **Weekly Holidays**

* At least **one day off** each week.

### **Leaves**

* **Casual Leave:** 10 days per year.
* **Sick Leave:** 14 days with wages.
* **Annual Leave:** 1 day leave for every 18 days of work (varies by industry).
* **Maternity Leave:** 16 weeks (8 weeks before and 8 weeks after childbirth).

---

## 5. Industrial Relations

### **Trade Union**

* Workers have the right to form or join a **trade union**.
* A minimum of **20% of workers** in an establishment is required to register a union.
* Registered unions have legal recognition to negotiate with employers.

### **Collective Bargaining Agent (CBA)**

* The CBA represents workers in:

  * Negotiating wages.
  * Working conditions.
  * Settlement of disputes.

### **Workers' Participation**

* Participation Committee must be formed in establishments with **50+ workers** to promote cooperation.

---

## 6. Disputes & Benefits

### **Dispute Resolution Mechanisms**

1. **Bipartite Negotiation:** Direct discussion between employer and worker representatives.
2. **Conciliation:** Labor authorities intervene to help reach an agreement.
3. **Arbitration:** A neutral arbitrator gives a binding decision.
4. **Labor Court & Appellate Tribunal:** Handles disputes related to wages, wrongful termination, and compensation.

### **Workers’ Participation in Company Profits**

* Companies must establish **Workers’ Profit Participation Fund (WPPF)**.
* Mandatory for companies established under the Companies Act.
* **5% of net profit** must be shared among employees.
* Promotes employee welfare and increases productivity.

---

## Summary

* The Bangladesh Labor Act, 2006 ensures worker protection, fair employment practices, and improved industrial relations.
* Key areas include safety, working hours, leave policies, adolescent employment, dispute resolution, and profit participation.
* The Act balances the rights and responsibilities of both workers and employers to maintain a healthy workplace environment.
