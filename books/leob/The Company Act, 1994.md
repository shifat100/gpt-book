# **Chapter 4: The Company Act, 1994**

The **Company Act, 1994** governs the formation, management, regulation, and winding up of companies in Bangladesh. It outlines the legal framework under which companies operate, ensuring transparency, accountability, and efficiency.

---

## **1. Overview of Companies**

### **Definition**

A **company** is an artificial legal person created by law, having perpetual succession and a common seal.

### **Characteristics of a Company**

* **Separate Legal Entity**
* **Limited Liability** of shareholders
* **Perpetual Succession**
* **Transferability of Shares**
* **Common Seal** (now optional)
* **Capacity to Sue and Be Sued**

### **Types of Companies**

#### **A. Private Limited Company (Ltd.)**

* Minimum **2** members; maximum **50** members.
* Restriction on transfer of shares.
* Cannot issue prospectus to the public.
* Must have at least **2 directors**.

#### **B. Public Limited Company (PLC)**

* Minimum **7** members; no maximum limit.
* Shares are freely transferable.
* Can issue prospectus for public subscription.
* Must have at least **3 directors**.

---

## **2. Key Documents of a Company**

### **A. Memorandum of Association (MOA)**

This document defines the **scope and primary objectives** of the company.

#### **Contents / Clauses**

1. **Name Clause** – Name of the company ending with Ltd./PLC.
2. **Registered Office Clause** – Location of company office in Bangladesh.
3. **Object Clause** – Main and ancillary objectives.
4. **Liability Clause** – Liability of members limited by shares/guarantee.
5. **Capital Clause** – Authorized share capital.
6. **Association/Subscription Clause** – Signatures of initial subscribers.

---

### **B. Articles of Association (AOA)**

The AOA contains internal rules for management.

#### **Purpose**

* Regulates internal operations.
* Guides directors and members.

#### **Contents**

* Share transfer procedures
* Meetings and voting rights
* Powers and duties of directors
* Dividend policy
* Borrowing powers

#### **Alteration of AOA**

* Done by a **special resolution**.
* Must be filed with the Registrar.

---

## **3. Incorporation of a Company**

### **Steps in Registration (RJSC Bangladesh)**

1. **Name Clearance** from RJSC.
2. Drafting **MOA and AOA**.
3. Prepare required documents:

   * Form I (Declaration)
   * Form VI (Particulars of Directors)
   * Form IX (Consent of Directors)
   * Information of registered office
4. **Submit documents** to RJSC.
5. **Pay registration fees** based on authorized capital.
6. RJSC reviews and issues:

   * **Certificate of Incorporation** (for both private and public companies)

### **Certificate of Incorporation**

* Acts as **birth certificate** of the company.
* Company becomes a **legal entity** from this date.
* Conclusive evidence of incorporation.

---

## **4. Prospectus**

A prospectus is a document inviting the public to purchase shares or debentures of a public company.

### **Purpose**

* To inform potential investors.
* Ensures transparency.

### **Contents**

* Company details
* Capital structure
* Objects of the issue
* Directors’ information
* Financial statements
* Auditor’s report
* Risk factors

---

## **5. Management & Directors**

### **Chairman**

* Presides over board meetings.
* Ensures compliance and policy implementation.

### **Directors**

Persons elected by shareholders to manage company affairs.

#### **Appointment**

* First directors named in AOA.
* Others appointed in AGM.

#### **Qualifications**

* Must be at least 18 years old.
* Not declared insolvent or of unsound mind.
* Must hold qualification shares (if required).

#### **Powers of Directors**

* Manage company business.
* Issue shares, borrow funds.
* Call meetings.
* Appoint officers.

#### **Duties**

* Act in good faith.
* Use powers for company benefit.
* Avoid conflict of interest.

#### **Removal**

* Can be removed by **ordinary resolution** with proper notice.

#### **Vacation of Office**

Occurs if the director:

* Becomes insolvent
* Becomes of unsound mind
* Fails to attend meetings for 3 months
* Resigns

#### **Remuneration**

* As per AOA or resolution of shareholders.

### **Managing Director (MD)**

* Chief executive of daily operations.
* Appointed by Board of Directors.
* Must be a director.

---

## **6. Meetings of a Company**

### **A. Statutory Meeting**

* Mandatory for public companies only.
* Held once within 1–6 months of incorporation.

### **B. Annual General Meeting (AGM)**

* Must be held **once every year**.
* Maximum gap between two AGMs: **15 months**.
* Business: approval of accounts, appointment of auditors, dividends.

### **C. Extraordinary General Meeting (EGM)**

* Called to discuss urgent matters.
* Can be called by board or by requisition of members.

---

## **7. Proceedings of Meetings**

### **Resolutions**

1. **Ordinary Resolution** – Passed by simple majority.
2. **Special Resolution** – Passed by 75% majority.

### **Minutes of Proceedings**

* Written record of decisions.
* Must be signed by chairman.
* Maintained in minute books.

---

## **8. Winding Up of a Company**

Winding up is the process of dissolving the company and distributing assets.

### **A. Winding Up by the Court**

Occurs when:

* Company unable to pay debts.
* Number of members falls below minimum.
* Court determines it is just and equitable.

### **B. Voluntary Winding Up**

* Initiated by members.
* Requires special resolution.

### **C. Winding Up Subject to Supervision of Court**

* Court supervises voluntary winding up.

### **D. Official Liquidator**

* Appointed by the court.
* Takes control of company assets.
* Settles liabilities and distributes remaining assets.
