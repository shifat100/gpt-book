# **Chapter 1: The Contract Act, 1872**

The **Contract Act, 1872** is one of the foundational laws governing commercial and personal transactions in Bangladesh. It sets out the rules for making, performing, and enforcing contracts. A clear understanding of this Act is essential for business students, entrepreneurs, and professionals.

---

## **1. Definition of a Contract**

According to Section 2(h) of the Contract Act, 1872:

> **“A contract is an agreement enforceable by law.”**

### **Essential Elements of a Valid Contract**

A valid contract must include:

* **Offer and Acceptance**: There must be a lawful offer and lawful acceptance.
* **Intention to Create Legal Relationship**: Parties must intend legal obligations.
* **Lawful Consideration**: Something of value must be exchanged.
* **Free Consent**: Consent must be free of coercion, fraud, etc.
* **Capacity of Parties**: Parties must be legally competent.
* **Lawful Object**: Purpose must not be illegal.
* **Certainty**: Terms must be clear.
* **Possibility of Performance**: Performance must be possible.
* **Legal Formalities**: If required, written form, registration, or stamping.

---

## **2. Formation of a Contract**

### **Offer (Proposal)**

* Defined under Section 2(a).
* Clear, communicated, and intended to create legal relations.

### **Acceptance**

* Defined under Section 2(b).
* Must be absolute, unconditional, and communicated in time.

### **Consideration**

* Defined under Section 2(d).
* Means “something in return” (quid pro quo).
* Can be past, present, or future.

Example:
A sells a phone to B for Tk. 5,000.

---

## **3. Types of Agreements**

### **Void Agreements**

Not enforceable by law. Examples:

* Agreements with minors.
* Mistake of fact by both parties.
* Agreements with unlawful object.

### **Voidable Agreements**

Enforceable only at the option of one party. Examples:

* Coercion.
* Undue influence.
* Fraud or misrepresentation.

### **Difference**

* **Void**: No legal effect.
* **Voidable**: Valid until cancelled.

---

## **4. Capacity of Parties**

Under Section 11, competent parties include:

* **Persons of Majority Age**: 18 years (21 if guardian appointed).
* **Persons of Sound Mind**.
* **Persons Not Disqualified by Law**.

Not competent:

* Minors.
* Persons of unsound mind.
* Bankrupts (in certain cases).

---

## **5. Free Consent**

Consent must not be caused by:

### **Coercion (Section 15)**

Threat or force.

### **Undue Influence (Section 16)**

Dominating will to gain unfair advantage.

### **Fraud (Section 17)**

Intentional deception.

### **Misrepresentation (Section 18)**

Innocent false statement.

### **Mistake (Sections 20–22)**

* Both parties mistaken: contract void.
* One party mistaken: not voidable.
* Mistake of law: not voidable.

---

## **6. Legality of Object and Consideration**

A contract is valid only if the object and consideration are lawful.

Unlawful when:

* Forbidden by law.
* Fraudulent.
* Causes injury.
* Immoral or against public policy.

Example: Selling illegal drugs = void.

---

## **7. Contingent Contracts**

### **Definition (Section 31)**

A contract dependent on a future uncertain event.

Example: A will pay B Tk. 10,000 if B’s house burns.

### **Rules (Sections 32–36)**

* Based on happening: enforceable only if event happens.
* Based on non-happening: enforceable when impossible.
* Event must be collateral.

---

## **8. Performance & Discharge of Contracts**

### **Performance**

* Actual performance.
* Attempted performance (tender).

### **Discharge Methods**

* Performance.
* Mutual agreement.
* Impossibility.
* Lapse of time.
* Operation of law.
* Breach.

### **Termination**

Contract ends if:

* Purpose achieved.
* Parties end it.
* Breach occurs.
* Performance becomes impossible.

---

## **9. Special Contracts**

### **A. Indemnity & Guarantee**

#### **Indemnity (Section 124)**

* 2 parties.
* Compensation for loss.
* Example: Insurance.

#### **Guarantee (Section 126)**

* 3 parties (Creditor, Principal Debtor, Surety).
* Surety liable if debtor defaults.

#### **Difference**

* Indemnity: liability arises only after loss.
* Guarantee: surety has secondary liability.

---

### **B. Bailment and Pledge**

#### **Bailment (Section 148)**

* Delivery of goods for specific purpose.
* Parties: Bailor, Bailee.
* Duties: reasonable care, return goods, disclose defects.

#### **Pledge (Section 172)**

* Bailment of goods as security.
* Parties: Pawnor, Pawnee.
* Pawnee may retain goods until payment.
* Pawnor may redeem after payment.
