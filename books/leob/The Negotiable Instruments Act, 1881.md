# **Chapter 2: The Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881**

The **Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881** governs the rules related to documents used in financial and commercial transactions, such as **promissory notes, bills of exchange, and cheques**. These instruments play an essential role in modern business by facilitating credit, payments, and trade.

---

## **1. Definition of Negotiable Instruments**

According to Section 13 of the Act:

> **A Negotiable Instrument means a promissory note, bill of exchange, or cheque payable either to order or to bearer.**

### **Essential Features**

* **Freely Transferable**: Can be transferred by delivery or endorsement.
* **Title Passes to Transferee**: Holder gets good title even if previous title was defective (holder in due course).
* **Presumption of Consideration**: Consideration is legally presumed.
* **Right to Sue**: Holder can sue in their own name.
* **Certainty of Amount & Time**: Specific sum of money; payable on demand or a fixed date.

---

## **2. Types of Negotiable Instruments**

### **A. Promissory Note (Section 4)**

A **written unconditional promise** by one person to pay another person a definite sum of money.

#### **Characteristics**

* Must be **in writing**.
* Must contain an **unconditional promise to pay**.
* Must be **signed by the maker**.
* Amount must be certain.
* Payable to a certain person or bearer.

**Example:**
“I promise to pay Rahim Tk. 10,000 on demand. — Signed: Karim”

---

### **B. Bill of Exchange (Section 5)**

A **written order** from one person to another to pay a certain sum of money to a third person.

#### **Characteristics**

* Contains an **order** (not request).
* Drawn by the **drawer**, directed to **drawee**, payable to **payee**.
* Must be **in writing and signed**.
* Amount must be certain.
* Acceptances required for validity (except cheques).

**Example:**
A orders B to pay C Tk. 50,000.

---

### **C. Cheque (Section 6)**

A **bill of exchange drawn on a banker** and payable on demand.

#### **Characteristics**

* Drawn on a **bank only**.
* Payable **on demand**.
* Must be written and signed.
* Includes **bearer, order, crossed, and account-payee** cheques.

---

## **3. Process of Acceptance and Negotiation**

### **A. Acceptance**

* Applicable mainly to **Bills of Exchange**.
* Drawee signs the bill to show acceptance.
* Acceptance can be:

  * **General Acceptance**: Without conditions.
  * **Qualified Acceptance**: With conditions (amount, time, place changes).

### **B. Negotiation**

* Transfer of instrument from one person to another.
* Done by:

  * **Delivery** (for bearer instruments)
  * **Endorsement + Delivery** (for order instruments)

### **Types of Endorsements**

* **Blank Endorsement**: Signature only.
* **Full Endorsement**: Pay to a specific person.
* **Restrictive Endorsement**: Limits further negotiation.
* **Conditional Endorsement**: With conditions.

---

## **4. Rights and Liabilities of Parties**

### **Parties in a Promissory Note**

* **Maker**: Primary liability.
* **Payee**: Right to receive payment.

### **Parties in a Bill of Exchange**

* **Drawer**: Creates the bill.
* **Drawee/Acceptor**: Pays the amount.
* **Payee**: Receives payment.

### **Parties in a Cheque**

* **Drawer**: Account holder.
* **Drawee Bank**: Bank on which cheque is drawn.
* **Payee**: Receiver of money.

### **Liabilities**

* **Primary Liability**: Maker (promissory note), Acceptor (bill), Drawee bank (cheque).
* **Secondary Liability**: Drawer and endorsers.
* **Holder in Due Course** gets special rights:

  * Better title even over defective titles.
  * Can enforce payment against all prior parties.

---

## **5. Dishonor of Negotiable Instruments**

Dishonor occurs when the instrument is not accepted or not paid.

### **A. Dishonor by Non-Acceptance**

* Drawee refuses to accept the bill.
* Drawee cannot be found.
* Insolvency of drawee.

### **B. Dishonor by Non-Payment**

* Drawee/bank refuses payment.
* Insufficient funds.
* Payment stopped by drawer.
* Signature mismatch.
* Cheque expired (valid for 6 months).

### **Consequences of Dishonor**

* **Notice of Dishonor** must be given to all prior parties.
* Holder may file a **suit for recovery**.
* In case of cheques:
  Dishonor for insufficiency of funds is an offence under the **Negotiable Instruments (Amendment) Act**, with penalties.
