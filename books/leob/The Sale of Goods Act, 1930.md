# **Chapter 3: The Sale of Goods Act, 1930**

The **Sale of Goods Act, 1930** governs commercial transactions involving the sale and purchase of movable goods. It defines key terms, outlines the formation of a contract of sale, and establishes rules for transfer of ownership, delivery, and warranties.

---

## **1. Definitions**

### **Buyer (Section 2(1))**

A **buyer** is a person who buys or agrees to buy goods.

### **Seller (Section 2(13))**

A **seller** is a person who sells or agrees to sell goods.

### **Goods (Section 2(7))**

**Goods** include every kind of movable property except:

* Money
* Actionable claims

Goods include:

* Stock and shares
* Growing crops
* Grass and things attached to land that can be severed

---

## **2. The Contract of Sale**

A contract of sale involves the transfer of property (ownership) in goods from the seller to the buyer for a price.

### **Sale**

* Immediate transfer of ownership from seller to buyer.
* Executed contract.
* Buyer becomes owner instantly.

### **Agreement to Sell**

* Ownership will transfer at a **future date** or **upon fulfilment of a condition**.
* Executory contract.
* Risk generally remains with seller until sale becomes complete.

### **Key Distinction**

| Basis                    | Sale                     | Agreement to Sell               |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------ | ------------------------------- |
| Transfer of Ownership    | Immediate                | Future date/condition           |
| Risk                     | Passes to buyer          | Remains with seller             |
| Nature                   | Executed                 | Executory                       |
| Rights in case of breach | Seller can sue for price | Seller can sue for damages only |

---

## **3. Transfer of Ownership (Property in Goods)**

The rules depend on the nature of goods:

### **A. Specific Goods**

* Ownership passes when the contract is made, if goods are in deliverable condition.

### **B. Unascertained Goods**

* Ownership transfers only after goods are **ascertained** or **appropriated** to the contract.

### **C. Goods Sent on Approval or Sale-or-Return**

Ownership passes when:

* Buyer approves or accepts the goods.
* Buyer keeps the goods beyond the fixed time.

### **D. Risk Follows Ownership**

* Unless otherwise agreed, **risk passes with property**.

---

## **4. Performance of the Contract of Sale**

### **A. Delivery of Goods**

Delivery means voluntary transfer of possession.

### **Types of Delivery**

* **Actual Delivery**: Physical handing over of goods.
* **Constructive Delivery**: When a third party acknowledges holding goods for the buyer.
* **Symbolic Delivery**: Transfer of a symbol (e.g., keys of a warehouse).

### **Rules of Delivery**

* Must be made at a reasonable hour.
* Seller must bear cost of delivery unless agreed otherwise.
* Goods must match the contract description.
* Buyer must accept delivery.

---

## **5. Conditions and Warranties**

In a contract of sale, certain **implied conditions and warranties** automatically apply unless excluded.

### **A. Implied Conditions**

* **Condition as to Title**: Seller has the right to sell the goods.
* **Condition as to Description**: Goods must match the description.
* **Condition as to Quality or Fitness**: Applies when buyer relies on seller's skill.
* **Condition as to Merchantability**: Goods must be fit for ordinary use.

### **B. Implied Warranties**

* **Warranty of Quiet Possession**: Buyer will not be disturbed.
* **Warranty of Freedom from Encumbrances**: Goods are free from charges.
* **Warranty as to Quality**: If defect is not apparent and known to seller.
